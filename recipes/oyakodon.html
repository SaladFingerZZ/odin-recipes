Oyakodon

Parent And Child Rice Bowl

Classic soul food of Japan, the literal translation of Oyakodon (親子丼) means “parent-and-child rice bowl.” Chicken (as in parent), egg (as in child), and onions are simmered together in a dashi and soy sauce-based broth and then served on top of steamed rice!

Ingredients

  • 1/2 pound boneless skinless chicken thighs
  • 1/2 onion,thinly sliced
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tsp dashi
  • 3 1/2 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 Tbsp sake
  • 2 Tbsp mirin
  • 1 1/2 Tbsp sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 2 cups cooked white rice(sushi rice)

Instructions

  1. Boil one cup of water and add dashi
  2. In a small mixing bowl, combine cooled dashi, soy sauce, sake, mirin and sugar. Mix until sugar is dissolved, set aside.
  3. Dice chicken thighs into 1-inch bite sized pieces, set aside.
  4. Whisk 2 eggs in a small bowl and set aside.
  5. Heat a small skillet over medium heat. Add half of the sliced onions and half of the diced chicken.
  6. Pour about 1/3 cup to 1/2 cup of prepared sauce into skillet.
  7. Once sauce comes to a boil, lower heat to medium low. cover with lid.
  8. Cook onions until softened and chicken is just cooked through, approximately 5 minutes.
  9. Slowly pour the eggs over the chicken and onions. Cover with lid and cook until eggs are just set. Do not overcook the eggs!
  10. Carefully pour the chicken and egg mixture over 1 cup of cooked rice.
  11. Repeat with remaining ingredients for a second bowl etc. serve while hot.
